I am a Research Fellow in computational physics, working on the numerical and theoretical investigation of disordered systems. My research focuses on understanding the structural, mechanical, and dynamical properties of amorphous materials using large-scale simulations.

My work explores problems such as vibrational properties of disordered solids, the emergence of the boson peak, relaxation dynamics in supercooled liquids, and the role of localized excitations in governing material response. I primarily use molecular dynamics simulations combined with statistical analysis to uncover connections between microscopic structure and macroscopic behavior.

During my doctoral and postdoctoral research, I have worked on identifying vibrational defects in amorphous solids, developing algorithms for disordered mass-spring networks, and studying relaxation processes in complex energy landscapes. My work aims to bridge the gap between atomistic simulations and continuum descriptions of materials.

I am also interested in developing computational tools and machine learning approaches to analyze complex physical systems and extract meaningful physical insights.
